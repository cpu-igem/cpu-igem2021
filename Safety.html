PROLOGUE

Safety is one of the most noteworthy issues in experiments. Therefore, CPU_CHINA has implemented several laboratory criteria to ensure the biosafety and biosecurity of our experiments.

GENERAL SAFETY IN LABORATORY

This year, all of our experiments are performed in BSL-1 laboratory. Before entering the laboratory, we are required to pass laboratory safety knowledge examination in order to establish basic cognitions on risk assessment, safety equipment, microbiological knowledge, emergency procedures and so forth. Also, before conducting experiments, we need to accept indispensable laboratory training regarding specific reagent and instrument usage. In this way, general laboratory knowledge, common cautions and safety awareness were educated.

Fig. 1 Laboratory safety education manual

In addition, during the experiments, we strictly obey related laboratory regulations and experiment protocols. All team members are required to wear gloves, masks and lab coats. Eating, drinking and smoking in the lab are forbidden.

Fig. 2 Masks, gloves and lab coats

At the same time, given that we need to carry out many microbiological experiments, aseptic procedures should be standardized:

  • Gloves must be disinfected with 75% ethanol before conducting aseptic operations.
  • All aseptic manipulations are carried out beside lighted alcohol lamps.
  • Every culture medium is treated with high pressure steam sterilization in order to avoid contamination.
  • Ultra clean bench will be exposed to UV light for more than 30 minutes for disinfection before use.

Fig. 3 Ultra clean bench

After experiments, with regard to the treatment of the waste, all waste bins or tanks are grouped into categories and placed in specified locations. The waste will be disposed of or recycled according to corresponding regulations.

Fig. 4 Experimental waste bins

SAFETY IN EXPERIMENT DESIGN

Considering biosafety, we choose Escherichia coli DH5alpha, TOP10, BL21(DE3), Rosetta(DE3) and Pichia pastoris GS115 as our chassis, as these organisms are categorized in risk group 1 and the iGEM white list, thus they are non-pathologic to human beings. The PE-degraded elements produced by our engineered strains, i.e., MnP, AAO and HFB1, are derived from risk group 1 organisms and have no toxicity, so they will not do harm to our health and environment. Also, for assembling our PE-degradation system, we use CRISPR/dCas9 technique containing deactivated CRISPR-associated protein 9 which does not possess a DNA-cleavage capacity, and three designed single guided RNAs (sgRNAs) which do not target human genes. Therefore, no risk of human genome damage can be caused by our CRISPR/dCas9 system.

Meanwhile, although being reprogrammed, the introduced parts will not confer our engineered strains a strengthened environmental adaptive capacity and make them become more advantageous in species competition. Thus, these engineered strains post little risk to the environment. Despite all this, in view of standard aseptic operation that we undertake, the risk of engineered strains escaping or leaking into the environment is minimized.

SAFETY IN HUMAN PRACTICES

Because of the COVID-19 pandemic, when visiting specific areas and people, the members of human practices were required to wear masks to decrease the risk of infection. All of the field trips and visits obeyed local epidemic prevention standards.

We contacted all companies and individuals involved in the interview in legal ways. The investigation and interview were conducted with their informed consent. At the same time, the members of Human Practices promised not to disclose the personal privacy and trade secrets of interviewees in any means.
